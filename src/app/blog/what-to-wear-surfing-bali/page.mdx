<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">The Rash Guard — Non-Negotiable for Beginners</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Almost every first-time surfer underestimates the rash guard. Without one, two hours of lying chest-down on a soft-top board, paddling, and pushing up into a pop-up will leave a raw red strip down your sternum and the inside of your forearms. It's not dangerous — but it stings in salt water for the rest of your trip. A rash guard makes that impossible.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Sleeves:</strong> long-sleeve. Short-sleeve rash guards exist, but for Bali sun and a 2-hour lesson the long sleeve protects your shoulders, upper arms, and the back of your neck — the parts that burn first. UPF 50+ is standard.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Fit:</strong> snug but not compressing. A loose rash guard balloons in the water and slows your paddling. Too tight restricts shoulder rotation. If it feels like a soft second skin, it's right.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Color:</strong> bright is better. Coral, lime, sky blue — anything visible. From the beach, your instructor (and your travel partner with the camera) need to spot you fast. Black rash guards disappear against the wave shadow at Batu Bolong.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Don't have one? <strong className="text-white">Every lesson I run includes a rash guard at no extra cost.</strong> If you want your own, every Canggu surf shop sells decent ones for around 200–350k IDR ($13–$23 USD).
</p>

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">Swimsuit — What Actually Stays On</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  This is where most beginners trip up. The bikini you brought for the pool will not survive your first wipeout in Batu Bolong's whitewash. Here's how to pick gear that stays where you put it.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">For women:</strong> a sport bikini with thick straps and tied bottoms, a sport one-piece, or board shorts with a sport bra/crop top. Avoid string ties at the back of the neck — they untie themselves on a duck-dive. Avoid bandeau tops without straps. If your favorite swimsuit has any "could come undone" elements, double-tie or save it for the pool.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">For men:</strong> board shorts with a drawstring you can actually tighten — and tie them tight. Loose surf shorts get pulled down constantly when the whitewash hits. The board-short length doesn't matter for performance; pick whatever you'd wear all day.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">For kids:</strong> a one-piece for girls, board shorts and a rash top for boys. Kids drift, fall, and get hit by surface waves more than adults — secure swimwear matters more, not less.
</p>

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">Reef-Safe Sunscreen — The One Thing People Get Wrong</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Bali sun is intense. We surf at Batu Bolong starting around 7am to beat the worst of it, but you're still exposed for 2 hours over open water that reflects UV straight into your face. Sunscreen isn't optional.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Use mineral, not chemical.</strong> Zinc oxide and titanium dioxide are reef-safe. Oxybenzone and octinoxate — the standard ingredients in most spray sunscreens — are banned in Hawaii, Palau, and increasingly in Bali resorts because they bleach coral. Even if enforcement is loose, it's the right call. Rocky's reef is what makes the wave at Batu Bolong work in the first place; protect it.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Apply 20 minutes before paddling out.</strong> Mineral sunscreen needs time to bind. If you slap it on at the beach and run into the water, half of it washes off in the first wave. Apply at the villa or in the car, then a top-up before entering the water.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Cover the spots people miss:</strong> tops of the ears, back of the neck, the strip between the rash guard and shorts when you arch up to paddle, the back of your knees, and the tops of your feet if you're not wearing booties. After 90 minutes paddling face-up to the sun, those spots are the first to burn.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Brands that work in Bali:</strong> Stream2Sea, Raw Elements, Sun Bum Mineral, Suntribe, Manda. Most are sold in Canggu's eco-shops and pharmacies. Avoid airport spray sunscreens — they're chemical and they wash off fast.
</p>

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">Wet Season vs Dry Season — Does It Change What to Wear?</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Mostly no. Bali water temperature barely shifts year-round — 27°C in dry season, 29°C in wet. The same swimsuit and rash guard work for both.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  The one adjustment is on the beach side. In wet season (November–March) afternoon storms are common, so bring a quick-dry bag and a small rain jacket if your lesson is later in the morning. In dry season (April–October) the sun is more direct — sunscreen reapplication matters more, and a UV-protective rash guard is non-negotiable. For more on which months are best, see <Link href="/blog/best-time-to-surf-canggu" className="text-accent-blue hover:underline">when to surf in Canggu</Link>.

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Can I wear leggings or yoga pants surfing in Bali?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  You can — surf leggings (UPF rated, snug fit) are popular for women who want extra sun coverage on the legs. Avoid cotton yoga pants; they balloon in the water and drag. Real surf leggings are sold in every Canggu surf shop for around 350–600k IDR.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Do I need surf booties in Bali?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  No. Batu Bolong's bottom is sand, and the water is warm. Booties are needed at reef breaks like Uluwatu or Padang Padang — not at a beginner-friendly beach break. If you're unsure where you'll be surfing, ask before buying anything.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Is it OK to surf in Bali on my period?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Yes — a tampon or menstrual cup is fine for a 2-hour lesson. Many of my students surf through their period without any issues. The water at Batu Bolong is busy with surfers all day, every day; you've got nothing to worry about.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">What should I wear if I'm a complete beginner who's never surfed?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  The same as everyone else — swimsuit, rash guard (we provide one), reef-safe sunscreen. The gear is identical for first-timers and pros. The difference is in the lesson, not the clothing. If you're brand new, my <Link href="/beginner-surf-lessons-bali" className="text-accent-blue hover:underline">beginner surf lessons in Bali</Link> page covers everything you need to know about your first session.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Should I tip my surf instructor in Bali?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Tipping isn't expected but is always appreciated for a great session. 50–100k IDR ($3–$7 USD) is generous for a 2-hour lesson. Cash works best — many instructors are independent and don't have card-payment setups.
</p>
