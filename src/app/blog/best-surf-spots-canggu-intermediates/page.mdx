  Canggu's coastline has roughly 4 km of surfable beach, and the five named breaks all share the same swell window — but each one suits a different stage of intermediate. The short version:
</p>

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">1. Batu Bolong — The Default Intermediate Wave</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Batu Bolong is the wave I teach 90% of my students on, beginner through intermediate, and it's also the first spot I'd send a visiting intermediate. The take-off zone is a long sandbar peak sitting in front of the reef-mouth (the "hole in the rock" the spot is named after), and the rides on a clean day stretch 80–120 metres on a single wave.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Why it works for intermediates:</strong> the wave is slow enough that you have time to set your line, stand up cleanly, and try a bottom turn or a cutback. It's not so slow that it dies on you — once you're in the open face, there's enough push to practice rail-to-rail surfing. The sand-and-reef bottom is forgiving compared to Uluwatu or Padang Padang, and wipeouts won't shred you.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">When to surf it:</strong> mid-tide rising is the sweet spot for the main peak. Low tide gets shallow and the wave gets faster — fun if you're confident, sketchy if you're still learning to read sections. High tide makes the wave fat and slow but less crowded. For full conditions I keep updated on my <Link href="/batu-bolong-surf" className="text-accent-blue hover:underline">Batu Bolong surf page</Link>.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Crowd warning:</strong> after 8am Batu Bolong becomes one of the busiest line-ups in Bali. 100+ surfers in the water on a holiday Tuesday is normal. If you hate crowds, paddle out at sunrise or shift to Pererenan.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Board:</strong> 7'0–7'6 funboard or longboard if you're working on glide and trim. Mid-length 7'2–7'10 is what most local intermediates ride here.
</p>

<div className="my-8 border-t border-border-subtle" />

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">2. Old Man's — The Forgiving Cousin</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Old Man's is 200 metres south of Batu Bolong, and the wave is named after the longboarders who've ridden it for decades. It breaks over a mostly-sand bottom with a softer shoulder than Batu Bolong, and the walls are mellower — easier to learn cutbacks on, harder to draw a tight line.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Why it works for early intermediates:</strong> if you can stand up consistently but you're still figuring out where to position on the wave face, Old Man's gives you more time to work it out. The peak shifts more than Batu Bolong, which forces you to read swell direction — a great skill-builder. There's also a left and a right going, so you get more wave-counts in a session.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">When to surf it:</strong> mid to high tide is best. Low tide can close out into shore. The wave likes a 1.0–1.8 m swell from the SW; on bigger days it gets walled up and confused.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Board:</strong> longboard or mid-length. Performance shortboards usually feel sluggish here.
</p>

<div className="my-8 border-t border-border-subtle" />

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">3. Echo Beach — The Step Up</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Echo Beach is the wave most Canggu intermediates dream about and most aren't ready for. It's a fast, occasionally hollow left-and-right reef break sitting north of Batu Bolong, and on the right swell it produces some of the cleanest barrels in Canggu. On the wrong swell it produces broken boards.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Why it's upper-intermediate:</strong> the take-off is steeper, the reef is shallower, and the wave doesn't forgive late drops. You need to commit to the wave or pull back early — there's no "I'll work it out on the way down." Crowds at peak hours are also intense, with locals and pros prioritizing the inside.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">When to surf it:</strong> mid-tide on a 1.5–2 m clean swell, before 9am if you're new to the spot. Watch a session from the beach first; the take-off zone is more specific than it looks. Avoid Echo on full-moon big swells unless you're confident on overhead waves.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Board:</strong> 6'6–7'0 funboard or step-up shortboard. Longboards are tolerated but you'll get burned on every set.
</p>

<div className="my-8 border-t border-border-subtle" />

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">4. Berawa — Punchy and Underrated</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Berawa sits south of Old Man's, in front of the row of beach clubs. It's a beach break with sand-bar peaks that shift week to week — sometimes there's a great A-frame, sometimes the line-up is closeouts. When it's working, it's punchier than Batu Bolong with proper open-face walls for turns.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Why it works for intermediates:</strong> Berawa rewards reading the bank. If you can identify which peak is breaking cleanest on the day, you can score waves with almost no one else on them — half the line-up is people who just paddled out without checking. It's also closer to most surf villas south of Canggu, so it's an easy second-session option.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">When to surf it:</strong> low to mid tide, smaller swells (1–1.5 m). Bigger days tend to close out across the bank.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Board:</strong> 6'4–7'2 funboard or shortboard depending on the day. Versatile boards win here.
</p>

<div className="my-8 border-t border-border-subtle" />

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">5. Pererenan — The Escape</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Pererenan is 5 minutes north of Echo, just past the river mouth. It's a mix of beach break and reef sections with a wide line-up — even on busy days you can find an empty peak. The wave isn't as long as Batu Bolong but the open shoulders are great for working on turns without 30 surfers cluttering the inside.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Why it works for intermediates:</strong> wide-open peaks let you commit to your line. The lower crowd density means you actually get the practice reps you came for. The drive in is short and parking is calm compared to Batu Bolong's chaos.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">When to surf it:</strong> mid-tide, 1–1.8 m swell. The reef sections show on lower tides, so be careful where you sit at low tide. Mornings only — afternoon onshores wreck Pererenan faster than the spots south of it.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  <strong className="text-white">Board:</strong> 6'8–7'4 funboard. Shortboards work on the punchier days.
</p>

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Can I rent a board at all five Canggu spots?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Yes — Batu Bolong, Old Man's, Echo, and Berawa all have multiple rental shacks 30 metres from the water. Pererenan has fewer but at least two shops at the main entrance. Daily rentals are 80–150k IDR for funboards, 150–250k IDR for performance shortboards. Bring a hotel ID for the deposit.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Are any of these spots dangerous for intermediates?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Echo Beach over a big swell is the only one I'd actively warn intermediates off. Batu Bolong, Old Man's, Berawa, and Pererenan are all rideable for someone who can paddle confidently and duck-dive head-high waves. The biggest hazard at all five is the crowd — collisions and dropped-in waves cause more injuries than the reef.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Should I drive a scooter between spots?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  All five spots are within 4 km of each other, so a scooter is the most efficient option. The roads between Berawa and Pererenan are tight and chaotic in peak season — drive defensively and check the swell forecast before leaving so you don't waste a drive on a flat spot. If you're not comfortable on a scooter, GoJek or Grab work for short hops.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">What's the best month to surf Canggu as an intermediate?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  May, June, September, and October are the cleanest months — dry season, consistent SW swell, predictable winds. July and August are the most popular but the most crowded. Wet season (November–March) still has surfable days, especially mornings, but expect more rain and shifting conditions. For a deeper breakdown see my <Link href="/blog/best-time-to-surf-canggu" className="text-accent-blue hover:underline">best time to surf in Canggu guide</Link>.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">What if I'm a beginner who thinks they're an intermediate?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Honest test: can you paddle into an unbroken green wave without a push, stand up on the open face, and ride it sideways for 5+ seconds? If yes, you're an intermediate. If you're still riding mostly whitewash, stay at Batu Bolong with a coach for a few more sessions before chasing Echo or Berawa.
