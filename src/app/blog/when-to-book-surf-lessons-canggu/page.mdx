<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Bali has two seasons — dry (May to September) and wet (November to March), with shoulder months on either side. For beginner surf lessons at Batu Bolong, here's how each block looks:
</p>

<ul className="space-y-3 text-text-secondary mb-6">
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-blue mt-1">★</span><span><strong className="text-white">May:</strong> arguably the single best month. Dry-season swells start filling in, crowds haven't ramped up, mornings are cool and glassy. Tide windows align well with sunrise sessions. Book 2–3 days ahead.</span></li>
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-blue mt-1">★</span><span><strong className="text-white">June:</strong> very similar to May. The wind picks up slightly in the afternoons but mornings stay clean. Slot availability is still relaxed.</span></li>
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-blue mt-1">⚠</span><span><strong className="text-white">July–August:</strong> peak everything. Strongest dry-season swells, biggest crowds, highest prices, hardest to get the 7 AM slot. Book 5–7 days ahead, especially for private lessons. If your fitness is good, the surf is at its most exciting; if you're cautious, May/September are calmer.</span></li>
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-blue mt-1">★</span><span><strong className="text-white">September:</strong> the second-best month after May. Crowds thin out, water stays warm, swell is still consistent. The mid-tide morning slots are particularly clean. Book 2–3 days ahead.</span></li>
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-blue mt-1">○</span><span><strong className="text-white">October:</strong> transition month — variable, leaning dry. Some of my favourite glassy days fall in October. Same-day booking usually works.</span></li>

  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-blue mt-1">○</span><span><strong className="text-white">April:</strong> wet-to-dry transition. Lots of mixed days but the good ones are excellent. Same-day booking.</span></li>

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">What Time of Day to Book Your Lesson</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Within any given day, when you book matters as much as which month. Bali's coastal weather has a predictable arc:
</p>

<ol className="space-y-3 text-text-secondary mb-6 list-decimal list-inside">
  <li><strong className="text-white">6:00–9:30 AM — peak window.</strong> Land is cooler than ocean, so the wind blows offshore (out to sea), holding the waves up cleanly. Sun is gentle. Line-up is mostly local longboarders and a handful of early lesson groups. Coffee shops have just opened. This is when I run 80% of my beginner lessons.</li>
  <li><strong className="text-white">9:30 AM–12:00 PM — degrading.</strong> Sun strengthens, wind starts to swing onshore (from the sea toward land), wave shape gets bumpy. Crowds explode as the late-rising tourists arrive. Still surfable but visibly worse than 8 AM.</li>
  <li><strong className="text-white">12:00–3:00 PM — worst window.</strong> Strong onshore wind, brutal sun, packed line-up, lower energy. Lessons at this hour exist but I avoid them. If your coach offers you only this slot, it usually means peak demand.</li>
  <li><strong className="text-white">3:00–5:00 PM — recovery.</strong> Wind drops back off, crowds thin slightly as people leave for sundowner cocktails. Sun is less harsh. Reasonable second-best option after sunrise.</li>
  <li><strong className="text-white">5:00–6:30 PM — golden hour.</strong> Glassy, beautiful light, smaller crowd. Catch a 90-minute lesson here and you finish with a sunset rinse. Note: the tide may not cooperate every day.</li>
</ol>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  When a student asks me "what time should I book?", I always say <em>let me check the tide first</em>. The ideal window of 6:30–9:30 AM only works if the tide is in the right phase. On days when low tide falls at 11 AM, the "good window" might shift to 9–11 AM. On days when low falls at 5 AM, the best slot is 6–8 AM. <Link href="/surf-lesson-prices-bali" className="text-accent-blue hover:underline">Most Bali surf lesson prices</Link> are the same regardless of slot — so always optimise for conditions, not convenience.
</p>

  <li><strong className="text-white">After lesson 1,</strong> you decide together whether to do another tomorrow, a private upgrade, a rest day, or jump straight to lesson 2.</li>

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  In wet season — usually yes, with caveats. In peak dry season — usually no, especially for the morning slots. Even if you find a coach willing, you're getting whoever is free, which often means the least-booked coaches. Better to pre-message someone you've vetted, even an hour ahead, than to gamble on a walk-up.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Does it cost more to book a popular morning slot?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  No. Most Canggu coaches charge a flat per-lesson rate regardless of slot — the price depends on private vs group and lesson length, not start time. That makes the 7 AM slot the single best-value time of day. See <Link href="/blog/how-much-surf-lesson-cost-bali" className="text-accent-blue hover:underline">how much a surf lesson costs in Bali</Link> for the full price breakdown.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">What if it rains during my booked slot in wet season?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Light rain is fine — the water is warm and you're already getting wet. We surf through it. The actual deal-breakers are lightning (very rare in the morning) and strong onshore squall winds that flatten the wave. Your coach will check the morning forecast and message you if a reschedule is smarter than pushing through. Build a buffer day into your trip and you'll never lose a session.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Is there a downside to booking in peak season?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Crowds, mostly. July–August at Batu Bolong has 60+ surfers on the inside on a clean morning. For beginners that means more wave conflict, more priority confusion, and slightly less coaching attention if you're in a group. The waves themselves are bigger and more energetic, which is great for someone past lesson three but intimidating for a first-timer.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Can I book a sunset surf lesson at Batu Bolong?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Yes — late-afternoon lessons starting around 4:30 PM finish at golden hour and are some of the most beautiful sessions of the trip. The trade-off: tide may not be perfect, the wind has only just dropped, and a 5 PM start in December gives you maybe 90 minutes before dark. Sunset is a treat session, not your primary lesson; do mornings as your bread and butter.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  If you're a complete beginner, yes — commute up to Canggu for lessons. Uluwatu's reef breaks are unsuitable for beginners. The commute is 60–80 minutes each way; either rent a scooter, take a Grab, or book lessons in 2-lesson blocks to make the trip worthwhile.
