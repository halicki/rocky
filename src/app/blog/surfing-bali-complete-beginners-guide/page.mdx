<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">How Surf Lessons in Bali Actually Work</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  A standard beginner lesson at Batu Bolong is 2 hours, runs early morning or late afternoon (mid-day is sun-blasted and crowded), and includes a soft-top board, leash, and rashguard. Here's the typical flow:
</p>

<ol className="space-y-3 text-text-secondary mb-6 list-decimal list-inside">
  <li><strong className="text-white">10–15 minutes on the beach.</strong> Coach explains wave anatomy, board parts, the pop-up technique, and rip-current safety. You practise the pop-up on the sand 4–5 times.</li>
  <li><strong className="text-white">5 minutes paddling out</strong> to the inside section — waist-deep water, 30 metres from shore.</li>
  <li><strong className="text-white">60–80 minutes of pushed waves.</strong> Coach stands beside you, points your board at the beach, waits for a clean broken wave, gives you a push. You pop up, ride straight to the beach in whitewater, walk back out. Repeat 15–25 times.</li>
  <li><strong className="text-white">10 minutes of debrief on the beach.</strong> Coach reviews what went right, what to fix tomorrow.</li>
</ol>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Lesson 1 goal: stand up on a whitewater wave. Most students hit this by minute 30. Lesson 2 goal: stand up consistently and start steering left/right. Lesson 3 goal: attempt your first un-pushed wave — paddle into a wave with your own arms. Progress beyond lesson 4 is a matter of repetition, not technique. The <Link href="/blog/what-to-expect-first-surf-lesson-bali" className="text-accent-blue hover:underline">first-lesson walkthrough</Link> covers the format in granular detail if you want a minute-by-minute preview.

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Most first-timers ask whether to book a private or a group lesson. The honest answer: private for lesson 1, group is fine after.
</p>

<ul className="space-y-3 text-text-secondary mb-6">
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-blue mt-1">→</span><span><strong className="text-white">Private (1 student, 1 coach):</strong> 50–80 USD per 2 hours. The coach can push you into 20+ waves and correct your form between each. Maximum learning per hour. Best for lessons 1 and 2.</span></li>
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-blue mt-1">→</span><span><strong className="text-white">Group (3–4 students, 1 coach):</strong> 30–45 USD per 2 hours. You get fewer pushes and less feedback, but more rest between waves (helpful for tired shoulders), more peer learning, and a more social atmosphere. Best for lessons 3+.</span></li>
</ul>

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">When to Go — Best Months for a Beginner</h2>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Bali has two seasons. Dry season (May–September) is the classic answer: clean offshore winds in the morning, sunshine all day, reliable swell. Wet season (November–March) gets a bad rap but is actually fine for beginners — smaller, softer waves, half the crowd, slightly cheaper lessons, and warm 30-minute rain squalls rather than washed-out days. The shoulders (April, October) are arguably the best — dry-season conditions without peak-season crowds.
</p>

<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Avoid only the worst of January and February if you can — that's the deepest part of wet season with the highest chance of multi-day onshore wind. <h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">Common Beginner Mistakes That Cost You Days</h2>

<ul className="space-y-3 text-text-secondary mb-6">
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-coral mt-1">−</span><span><strong className="text-white">Surfing without a coach on day 1.</strong> Walking up to Batu Bolong, renting a foamie, and paddling out solo without a lesson is how 80% of self-taught beginners develop bad habits that take years to undo.</span></li>
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-coral mt-1">−</span><span><strong className="text-white">Booking too many back-to-back lessons.</strong> 5 lessons in 5 days is feasible only if you're in athletic shape.   <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-coral mt-1">−</span><span><strong className="text-white">Trying Uluwatu, Padang, or Bingin on the first trip.</strong> Those are reef breaks for advanced surfers. The "famous waves" of Bali are not where you learn.</span></li>
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-coral mt-1">−</span><span><strong className="text-white">Underestimating the sun.</strong> Equatorial sun + reflective water + 2 hours = a back blister by session 2 if you skip the rashguard or sunscreen.</span></li>
  <li className="flex gap-2 items-start"><span className="text-accent-coral mt-1">−</span><span><strong className="text-white">Booking through a 3rd-party tour desk.</strong> You'll pay 30–50% more and get a random coach. Message a local coach directly via WhatsApp.</span></li>

<h2 className="font-heading text-2xl font-bold text-white mt-10 mb-4">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Can I learn to surf in Bali if I'm over 40 or over 50?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Yes — easily. Batu Bolong sees plenty of students in their 50s, 60s, and occasionally 70s. The wave is soft enough, the boards are forgiving foam, and a good coach will pace the lesson to your shoulders. The honest caveat: paddling is the limiting factor at any age. If you can do 10 push-ups and swim 100 metres, you'll be fine.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Is it safe to bring my kids to surf in Bali?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Most coaches happily teach kids from age 7 upwards at Batu Bolong, and many take younger ones with a parent in the water. The wave shape is forgiving and the depth is friendly. See <Link href="/blog/canggu-surfing-safe-for-kids" className="text-accent-blue hover:underline">is Canggu surfing safe for kids</Link> for the full age-and-condition breakdown.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Do I need to know how to swim well to start surfing in Bali?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  You need to be comfortable in chest-deep water, comfortable with your head going under, and able to swim 50–100 metres unaided in case your leash snaps. You don't need to be a strong swimmer or have lap-pool training. If swimming is genuinely a fear, do a couple of pool sessions before the trip and start at Sanur on high tide rather than Canggu.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Should I buy a board for my Bali surf trip?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  No, not for trip 1. Rent. Coaches provide boards in lessons and rentals run 5–10 USD for a foamie. After your second trip, if you've decided this is a thing in your life, look at buying a 7'2 soft-top and either bringing it in luggage or storing it at your coach's place between trips.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">How fit do I need to be to learn surfing in Bali?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Moderately. Surfing uses shoulders, upper back, and core. If you can do 10–15 push-ups, hold a 1-minute plank, and swim 200 metres comfortably, you're ready. If you're way below that, do 4 weeks of push-ups and easy swimming before the trip and your lessons will be twice as productive.
</p>

<h3 className="font-heading text-xl font-semibold text-white mt-8 mb-3">Can I learn surfing in Bali by myself without lessons?</h3>
<p className="text-text-secondary leading-relaxed mb-4">
  Technically yes, practically no. Self-taught beginners spend years learning what a coach corrects in two hours, develop scary habits in line-up etiquette, and risk injuring themselves or others. At Canggu prices, lessons are too cheap to skip. Take at least 3 lessons before you start free-surfing on your own.
</p>
