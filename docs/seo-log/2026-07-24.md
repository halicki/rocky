### Honest caveat on the rule's rationale

Rule 4 is framed as a *CTR* rescue, and on CTR grounds the diagnosis would be wrong. At pos 27.5 expected CTR is roughly 0.2%; this page earns **0.5%**. Its title is **over**-performing for its position. Rewriting it to win more clicks at pos 27.5 would be attacking the wrong variable — precisely the misclassification corrected on 07-21.

The reason to rewrite is **relevance, not CTR**: the page's head-query signals point at a phrase with zero demand. Fixing that should move *position*, and clicks follow position. Rule 4 supplies the correct mechanism under an incorrect label. Recording this explicitly so a future run does not read "Rule 4 on a pos-27 page" as precedent for a genuine CTR rescue at depth.
